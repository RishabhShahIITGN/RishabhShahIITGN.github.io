About Me

I am a Junior Undergraduate pursuing a B.Tech. in Mechanical Engineering at IIT Gandhinagar, maintaining a CPI of 8.30. With a strong academic foundation in core mechanics, particularly in mathematics, statics, and dynamics. My engineering philosophy centers on bridging the gap between physical mechanisms and computational intelligence.

My fascination with mechanism design is deeply rooted in practical application. Having been exposed early on to the complexities of a mechanical spare parts manufacturing business, I developed a hands-on appreciation for industrial operations. This background drives my passion for optimizing modern autonomous industrial systems and developing robust solutions for real-world manufacturing environments.

Currently, my research and technical focus lies at the intersection of soft robotics, reinforcement learning, and sensor fusion. Whether I am developing autonomous forklift localization pipelines, exploring biomimetic design for soft surgical robots, or bridging sim-to-real gaps using ROS 2 and MuJoCo, I am driven by the challenge of designing machines that sense, decide, and move with high precision.

Beyond the technical execution, my approach to engineering is guided by a strict commitment to academic integrity and collaborative problem-solving. I thrive in team environments, actively assisting my peers, and contributing to a culture where innovative ideas are built.
